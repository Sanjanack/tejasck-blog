Hello everyone,

I’m writing this from my room in Schmalkalden, and it still feels a little unreal to say — I’m actually here.

## How did I even end up here?

A few months ago, Germany was just an idea. Something I would think about in between classes, assignments, and random conversations. As a mechanical engineering student, it always had a certain pull — the place where everything I studied actually exists beyond textbooks. Cars, machines, precision — not just concepts, but part of everyday life. You keep hearing about it — the Autobahns, the engineering, the systems — and at some point, it stops being just interesting and starts becoming a goal.

The journey from India to here didn’t happen in one big moment. It was a series of small steps — applications, documents, waiting, and a lot of second thoughts. And then suddenly, you’re on a flight. Landing in Frankfurt, watching the landscape from above — it felt like everything was finally real.

The train ride to Schmalkalden was probably when it truly hit me. Small towns, forests, quiet stations — everything looked different from what I was used to. And now, this is where I am.

Schmalkalden itself is not a place I had heard of before this journey. But it’s slowly becoming familiar. Walking through the town, figuring things out, adjusting to a new routine — it’s all new, but it feels like the right kind of new.

Of course, there are challenges. The weather is colder than anything I’ve experienced, and the language barrier is real. But at the same time, there’s a sense that this is exactly what I signed up for — something different, something outside comfort.

## What's next

This blog is just a simple record of how this journey happened. From thinking about Germany to actually making it here. I’ll be sharing the process, the requirements, and the steps involved — in a way that’s easy to follow if you’re planning something similar.

Nothing too detailed. Just the real experience.

This is where it begins.
