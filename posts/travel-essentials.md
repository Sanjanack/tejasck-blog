By the time you reach this stage, most of the hard work is done.

You have your admission, your visa, and your flight.

Now it’s about making sure you don’t miss anything important before leaving.

Here’s a simple checklist based on what actually matters.

---

## What documents should you carry?

Keep both **originals and copies**.

- Passport  
- Visa  
- University Admission Letter  
- APS Certificate  
- IELTS Certificate  
- Academic Documents (10th, 12th, Degree, Transcripts)  
- Health Insurance Proof  
- Blocked Account Confirmation  
- Accommodation Details  
- Flight Tickets  

Tip: Keep scanned copies on your phone and email as backup.

---

## What should you pack?

Don’t overpack — but don’t miss essentials.

### Clothes
- Winter wear (jackets, thermals, gloves)  
- Basic daily wear  
- Comfortable shoes  

### Essentials
- Medicines (with prescription if needed)  
- Basic toiletries  
- Power adapters (important)  

---

## Money & Setup

- Carry some cash in Euros  
- International debit/forex card  
- Keep access to your blocked account  

---

## What do people usually forget?

- Proper winter clothing  
- Multiple document copies  
- Power adapters  
- Basic medicines  

---

## One important tip

Start looking for accommodation as early as possible.  
It’s much easier to secure a place before arriving in Germany.

---

## Final thought

At this point, it can feel overwhelming.

But once you land, most of these things fall into place.

You don’t need everything to be perfect —  
just prepared enough to begin.

And that’s all this journey really needs.
